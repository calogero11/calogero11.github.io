Where my passion began...

When I was younger, I really underestimated how complex technology was and I kind of thought these things just ‘worked’... well, this was until I gave my first instruction to a computer; <p>Hello World</p> Where I realised the complexities of some sites and applications.

Since then I've been acknowledging the technology around us that developers have coded to help which have made our lives much easier and much more efficient.

With the acknowledgment comes understanding - which is why I decided to learn how to code. Initially it started with the help of teachers, tutorials and late nights where things just wouldn’t work and I would have no idea why. Looking back now in most cases it was python’s strict indentation, which I gathered a little later.

My passion for programming since I started has grown alongside my development and I think it always will. I know this because every time I learn something that I'd never done before I get an amazing feeling of accomplishment and growth which is why I’ve decided to fulfil my dream of becoming a developer.

Laravel, my biggest self-taught experience

So, to get ahead of the game, I decided to learn Laravel. Initially I was in doubt about Laravel and whether it was worth learning. However, I quickly realised it was.

I was trying to create a log in system and it was taking me hours. It was unreliable, unsecure and possibly even broken, well this was until I RTD; this is where I discovered that Laravel was all about code reusability, creating a log in system had never been so easy, it was as simple as typing: php artisan make:auth which created a secure system in a matter of seconds, at this point I fell in love with Laravel.

When I initially developed a website, I just kept all my website files in a single folder, it became hard to maintain and I never knew where to find files. With Laravel MVC’s nature all elements of a website are kept in logical order.

Also, let's not talk about artisan - how many times have you written a piece of code that you'll need to repeat in every other document in your project? Well let's just say that artisan does all that for you, why would you want to be a wet developer when you can be a dry one?

In conclusion, I think that Laravel is a great way to start learning programming and a great way to get prepared for the future as a developer.
